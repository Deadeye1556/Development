# CEO TASK BRIEF — COO — 2026-06-06

**MILESTONE:** Pre-Launch — Advertising and Hype Generation
**TASK:** Commission the operations team to begin pre-launch advertising and hype-building. Forkd needs a presence, an audience, and momentum before the app ships. This work starts now.
**PRIORITY:** P2 — High. M1 tech work is P1 and is not blocked by this. Operations runs this track in parallel. Every week without a pre-launch presence is audience we are not building.
**DUE:** Initial strategy and first deliverables by June 20, 2026. Ongoing through launch.

---

## Context

M1 (auth + profiles) is in device verification. The app is real enough to talk about. We have:
- A clear product identity: social platform for consumable crafting — food, ferments, mead, spirits, cider, homebrewing, distilling
- A creator revenue share program with real differentiation (badge permanent, rate requalifies monthly, no ceiling)
- A one-tap grocery cart integration that no social food app has
- A launch target of November 16, 2026

We need an audience waiting when we ship. That means starting now.

---

## What I Want From Operations

### 1. Pre-Launch Advertising Strategy (Advertising Specialist, led by PM)

The Advertising Specialist owns the answer to: where do we show up, what do we say, and how do we build an audience before the app is live?

Scope includes but is not limited to:
- Which channels to activate first (Instagram, TikTok, Reddit, YouTube, creator outreach, waitlist email, App Store pre-order page)
- What the brand voice and content angle is ("the app for people who make things you can eat, drink, or brew")
- Creator outreach plan — who are the 10–20 seed creators we want on the platform at launch, and how do we recruit them
- App Store pre-order or early access page copy and screenshots (M1 gives us enough to screenshot)
- Messaging that leads with the creator revenue angle, not just the recipe angle

### 2. Market and Channel Intelligence (Market Researcher, supporting)

Before Advertising Specialist commits to channels, Market Researcher should answer:
- Where does the homebrewing / craft ferment / food creator audience actually live online?
- Which competitors are advertising and what are they saying? What angles are unclaimed?
- What is the realistic pre-launch audience-building timeline for an app in this category?

### 3. Growth Infrastructure (Growth Engineer, supporting)

Before launch hype generates traffic, we need somewhere to capture it:
- Referral or waitlist mechanism — simple email capture or early access sign-up
- Attribution plan — how do we know which channel drove which installs at launch?
- Creator referral framing — how do we incentivize seed creators to bring their audience?

---

## Constraints

- **Do not position Forkd as a recipe app.** Position it as the platform for people who make consumable things — recipes are one category, not the whole story.
- **Creator revenue is the lead differentiator.** No other social food/drink app pays creators on grocery cart conversion. That is the hook.
- **Nothing launches publicly without CEO approval.** Strategy and copy are deliverables — execution of paid campaigns requires a separate CEO green light.
- **Grocery integration is a feature, not the lead.** It supports the creator revenue story; it is not the headline.

Cascade to Product Manager. PM assigns:
- Advertising Specialist: pre-launch advertising strategy (primary deliverable)
- Market Researcher: channel and competitor intelligence (supporting — feeds Advertising Specialist)
- Growth Engineer: waitlist/referral infrastructure plan (supporting — feeds launch execution)

Coordinate sequencing with PM. Market Researcher intelligence should inform Advertising Specialist strategy, so consider running Market Researcher first or in tight parallel.
