**OBJECTIVE:** App Store listing strategy, first draft copy, and keyword research

---

## Context

M6 (Polish & Beta) targets October 26, 2026. App Store submission happens during M6. The listing copy and keyword strategy need to be researched and drafted before we are in crunch. Start now while there is time to do this well.

Forkd's App Store differentiator: it is the only recipe app where content directly drives grocery purchases with creator revenue share. No other recipe app has this. The copy must lead with this, not bury it.

---

## Task 1 — Competitor App Store Research

Research these apps on the iOS App Store:
- Yummly, Tasty, Whisk, Paprika, Mela (recipe apps)
- BrewFather (craft beverage)
- Any app ranked #1–10 in Food & Drink category currently

For each, document:
- Title (exact, with character count)
- Subtitle (exact, with character count)
- First 3 lines of description (what appears before "More")
- What keywords appear to be targeted (infer from title/subtitle/description)
- App Store rating

Identify: what keywords appear in multiple top-ranked Food & Drink apps?

---

## Task 2 — Keyword Research

Draft the App Store keyword field (100 character limit, comma-separated, no spaces after commas).

Research which keywords to include:
- High-volume terms in Food & Drink: recipe, cooking, meal plan, grocery list
- Differentiated terms: homebrew recipes, mead recipes, fermentation, craft beverage
- Creator economy terms: recipe creator, food creator (do users search these?)
- Nutrition terms: nutrition tracker, macro tracker, calorie counter

Rationale required for each keyword group: why include it, what search intent it captures.

---

## Task 3 — Title Options (3 drafts)

App Store title: 30 character limit. This appears in search results and on the app icon.

Draft 3 options. Each must:
- Be under 30 characters (count them)
- Include at least one high-value keyword
- Be memorable and distinct from competitors
- Accurately represent the app

Lead with the craft/recipe angle OR the shopping angle — pick the stronger hook for each option and label your choice.

---

## Task 4 — Subtitle Options (3 drafts)

App Store subtitle: 30 character limit. Appears directly below the title in search results.

Draft 3 options. Each must:
- Be under 30 characters
- Lead with a benefit, not a feature
- Complement the title (don't repeat the same keywords)
- For at least one option: reference the grocery cart / shopping angle
- For at least one option: reference the creator earning angle

---

## Task 5 — Description First Paragraph

The first 3 lines of the App Store description appear before the user taps "More." This is conversion copy — it either hooks them or loses them.

Draft the first paragraph (aim for 3–4 lines visible on a standard phone screen). It must:
- Lead with the most powerful benefit (what does Forkd do for me?)
- Include the grocery cart hook (this is the differentiator)
- Mention the creator revenue angle
- Not use tech jargon
- Not make claims Forkd cannot deliver at M6 launch

---

## Task 6 — Screenshot Strategy Confirmation

Review the current 6-slot screenshot plan in your role.md. Based on your App Store research:
- Does the current plan need any updates?
- Which slot is most important for conversion?
- Any screen that competitors are NOT showing that Forkd should lead with?

Confirm or update the 6-slot plan with brief rationale for each slot.
